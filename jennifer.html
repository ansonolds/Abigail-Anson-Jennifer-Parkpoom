Asian Inspired Recipes

I created this recipe to mimic the taste of cold sesame noodles from our local Chinese food restaurant. It is easy to make at home and can be eaten warm or cold, making any leftovers perfect for a desk lunch the next day. You can serve these noodles with raw vegetables such as sliced cucumber, carrot sticks, and red pepper strips for a complete meal. Kids love these!

Peanut Sesame Noodles

By Jennifer Hibbins


Ingredients

  • 3/4 lb. spaghetti
  • 1/3 cup reserved cooking water
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp. ground ginger
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 2 Tbsp. rice vinegar
  • 1.5 Tbsp. sesame oil
  • 1 Tbsp. honey
  • Toasted sesame seeds (optional)


Steps

  1. Cook spaghetti noodles in salted water according to the package directions, reserving 1/3 cup of the hot cooking water.
  2. Whisk remaining ingredients with the reserved hot water to make the dressing.
  3. Toss noodles with dressing until well-combined.
  4. Sprinkle with sesame seeds, if desired.
  5. Serve warm or cold and enjoy!
